# World Bible

Status: Approved

## Purpose

This document defines the minimum playable world required to implement Irys+Ion.

The world exists to support:

- the Ark preparation loop;
- a fixed five-boss run;
- short connective stages;
- the planetary AI’s escalating defensive forms;
- the control-center false ending;
- the return to the Ark;
- the final confrontation and ending.

The World Bible should not expand into a complete planetary history or setting encyclopedia.

## World Scope

The game depicts two principal locations:

1. The Ark.
2. The planetary AI’s megastructure.

The Ark is physically separate from the megastructure.

The playable route through the megastructure is fixed and linear.

The broader planet may be suggested through distant views, environmental remnants, dialogue, or optional collectibles, but it does not need to be directly explored or fully explained.

The origin of the megastructure is intentionally unspecified.

It does not matter whether it was originally constructed by the planetary AI or inherited from an earlier civilization unless implementation later exposes a contradiction.

## The Ark

The Ark is a concealed creation facility built by Ion.

It is operated by the intelligence named Irys.

The Ark produces the physical Irys bodies capable of wielding Ion.

It contains thousands of dormant bodies held in pods.

The visible scale of the pod field should establish that the player-selected candidates are a small part of a much larger reserve.

The Ark is the location for:

- the beginning of every run;
- candidate generation;
- candidate inspection;
- Irys selection;
- Ion binding to the selected body;
- the concealed teleport departure;
- Ion’s return after run failure;
- the final confrontation;
- the ending.

The Ark should initially feel isolated, controlled, and safe.

Its violation during the final act should therefore carry immediate weight.

## Candidate Chamber

Before each run, three generated Irys candidates are displayed in active pods.

The player may inspect each candidate’s:

- visible body;
- anatomical variations;
- defects or anomalies;
- traits;
- derived gameplay statistics;
- meaningful strengths and disadvantages.

After one candidate is selected, the other two pods close.

The unselected Irys remain dormant.

The candidate chamber should retain recognizable visual continuity across runs.

At least one early unselected candidate should be visually memorable enough to be recognized when her pod opens during the ending.

## Teleport Platform

The Ark contains a concealed teleport platform.

The selected Irys and Ion use this platform to begin each run.

The platform sends them to a fixed ingress point near the outer layer of the planetary AI’s megastructure.

The destination relay, platform, or access point conceals itself after use through camouflage, cloaking, retraction, or another readable technological effect.

The exact teleportation mechanism does not require deeper explanation.

The link must establish:

- why every run begins at the same point;
- how the Ark remains physically separate from the megastructure;
- how Ion returns to the route;
- how the final return to the Ark is possible;
- how repeated use can eventually expose the Ark to the planetary AI.

## Ion’s Return After Failure

When an active Irys is defeated, Ion rises from the fallen body.

A brief visual transition implies Ion traveling back to the Ark.

The game does not need to depict the entire journey.

After the transition, the player returns to the Ark candidate chamber and begins the next preparation sequence.

## The Planetary Megastructure

The planetary AI occupies and controls a structure of planetary scale.

The playable route is primarily internal.

Most environments should feel like connected layers of one enormous system rather than unrelated regions.

Short exterior crossings, windows, breaches, shafts, or distant views may establish scale and provide visual contrast.

Exterior sections should remain limited and should not require large open-world environments.

The megastructure becomes increasingly refined, adaptive, and Irys-like as the player advances.

This environmental progression parallels the boss progression.

## Stage One: Industrial Perimeter

The first stage begins at the concealed ingress relay.

It occupies a crude industrial or service layer of the megastructure.

Possible environmental elements include:

- heavy machinery;
- exposed mechanisms;
- maintenance passages;
- large conduits;
- lifts;
- processing systems;
- crude defensive architecture.

The environment should support the first guardian’s mechanical and non-Irys-like design.

Traversal should be short.

Its primary functions are:

- establishing the scale and material language of the megastructure;
- introducing a small number of deliberate enemies;
- reinforcing basic use of The Language;
- leading efficiently to the first boss.

## Stage Two: Adaptive Infrastructure

The second stage is more articulated and responsive than the first.

The planetary AI’s systems should appear increasingly capable of observing, repositioning, manufacturing, or adapting.

Possible environmental elements include:

- active assembly systems;
- articulated machinery;
- responsive doors and partitions;
- automated transit;
- moving structural components;
- more refined defensive systems.

The stage should visually support the transition from crude machinery toward humanoid combat geometry.

Traversal remains short and boss-oriented.

## Stage Three: Biomechanical Experimentation Zone

The third stage shows the planetary AI moving toward imitation of the Irys form.

Technology and constructed anatomy begin to converge.

Possible environmental elements include:

- suspended body-like structures;
- incomplete limbs;
- articulated frames;
- restraints;
- support machinery;
- failed or unfinished humanoid forms;
- large-scale experimental chambers.

The third guardian is a large, recognizably Irys-like figure attached to, restrained against, or integrated into the environment.

The arena must support combat against its arms, legs, or both.

The environment should make the guardian feel like an incomplete experiment rather than a naturally occurring creature.

## Control Center

The control center is the planetary AI’s apparent core domain.

Its visual language should be more refined, precise, and technologically advanced than the outward stages.

It should feel controlled rather than cluttered.

The control-center boss is a formidable advanced mech or technological combat body.

The arena and approach should support the false impression that this is the final destination and the planetary AI’s ultimate form.

Defeating the control-center boss triggers the reveal that the planetary AI has traced the Ark.

## Return Route

After the control-center reveal, the current Irys and Ion must return to the concealed transit link.

The return route should be shorter and more urgent than the outward journey.

The player should not replay the full campaign in reverse.

The return may use:

- emergency access routes;
- opened shortcuts;
- bypassed systems;
- damaged infrastructure;
- altered portions of previous stages;
- rapid transit systems;
- collapsing or destabilized spaces.

The return sequence should preserve urgency without adding another full stage-length gauntlet.

The concealed teleport link then returns Irys and Ion to the Ark.

## Final Ark State

The Ark has been breached or compromised by the planetary AI.

The familiar candidate and pod spaces become the final battlefield.

The final encounter pits:

- the player’s current Irys wielding Ion;
- another created Irys wielding the planetary AI in blade-form.

The final arena should remain visibly recognizable as the Ark.

Pods and dormant Irys bodies should remain present so the stakes of the confrontation and the ending are spatially immediate.

The Ark’s familiar preparation space should not be replaced by an unrelated final arena.

## Environmental Activity Arc

The planetary megastructure must change in perceived activity across the campaign.

Early regions should feel:

- ancient;
- incomprehensibly vast;
- dormant or only partially active;
- larger than the player can understand from the visible route;
- filled with machinery whose complete purpose cannot be determined.

Scale should be communicated through:

- deep parallax layers;
- distant moving structures;
- enormous shafts and voids;
- machinery extending beyond the frame;
- limited exterior glimpses;
- inactive systems far larger than the playable foreground.

As the player advances, the megastructure becomes increasingly awake.

Later regions should reveal:

- active maintenance;
- self-repair;
- coordinated fabrication;
- moving assembly infrastructure;
- continuous manufacturing;
- precise environmental control;
- visible systems performing purposeful work.

The final one or two megastructure regions should feel fully active, maintained, and controlled rather than ruined.

The control center is not an ancient ruin. It is the planetary AI’s immaculate, functioning domain.

## Anti-Irys Containment Chamber

An adjoining chamber beyond the control-center command mech contains the inactive Anti-Irys substrate.

The chamber must visually establish:

- Anti-Irys as complete but inert;
- tendrils, restraints, a partial pod, or another support structure;
- the planetary AI’s energy distributed throughout the room and facility;
- a clear route through which that energy drains into Anti-Irys;
- the counterfeit blade manifesting only when the transfer completes.

The chamber should become visibly depleted and dark as the planetary AI abandons the megastructure.

Anti-Irys leaves the control-center region after activation. The player does not fight her there.

## Shutdown Return State

After the planetary AI transfers into its blade-form and departs with Anti-Irys, the megastructure enters a shutdown condition.

The return route should feel like movement through a facility whose coordinating intelligence has left it.

Environmental changes include:

- planetary-AI illumination disappearing;
- machinery halting, coasting, flickering, or failing;
- doors and transit systems operating irregularly;
- previously precise systems becoming unstable;
- familiar regions appearing inert or partially collapsed;
- emergency routes and shortcuts opening;
- existing enemies behaving without central coordination.

The return route remains shorter than the outward route.

It does not introduce a fourth environment tier or replay the complete campaign in reverse.

## Final Pod-Chamber Arena

The final confrontation occurs in the Ark’s main pod chamber rather than in an unrelated arena.

The player must remain surrounded by the visible dormant candidates that have defined the roguelite loop.

During scripted phase transitions, the Ark protects those candidates by:

- raising barriers;
- deploying shutters;
- retracting pod banks;
- shifting pods away from threatened areas;
- isolating a smaller protected cluster as the chamber becomes damaged.

These changes create authored combat geometry but never become an escort or defense objective.

Pod losses, damaged partitions, and protection failures are scripted rather than determined by player performance.

The final environment must make the Ark intelligence feel alive through its behavior. Its machinery, lighting, barriers, pod movement, and protective choices are its primary physical expression.

When the planetary-AI blade attempts to escape after Anti-Irys falls, the Ark itself prevents the return or teleportation attempt.

## Ending Environment

The final conflict critically compromises the Ark.

After the planetary AI is defeated, Irys and Ion merge and distribute their merged consciousness across the dormant created bodies.

Pods throughout the Ark begin opening.

The ending should include one of the earliest unselected candidates awakening.

The visible field of opening pods transforms the Ark from a body-production facility into the birthplace of many independent individuals.

## Traversal and Enemy Density

Connective stages are not the primary combat content.

They should contain a small number of deliberate encounters rather than long enemy waves.

Traversal exists to:

- introduce environments;
- rehearse mechanics;
- foreshadow bosses;
- deliver dialogue;
- provide pacing;
- create contrast between major fights.

Boss encounters should account for most active combat time.

Stages should not become resource-tax corridors before the primary encounters.

## Environmental Storytelling

Environmental storytelling should remain concise and legible.

The world may suggest:

- previous failed Ion infiltrations;
- the planetary AI’s defensive iteration;
- incomplete experiments;
- damage caused during earlier runs;
- the scale of the Ark’s dormant population;
- the gradual convergence toward the Irys form.

Optional lore collectibles may provide limited additional context.

They should not be required to understand the main story.

## Implementation Requirements

The implemented world must include:

- a physically separate Ark;
- a visible field of dormant Irys pods;
- a three-candidate preparation area;
- a concealed teleport departure platform;
- a fixed ingress relay at the megastructure;
- a readable concealment or cloaking effect after arrival;
- a primarily internal megastructure route;
- limited exterior views establishing planetary scale;
- a crude industrial first stage;
- a more adaptive and articulated second stage;
- a biomechanical experimental third stage;
- an environmentally integrated proto-Irys boss arena;
- a refined control center;
- an advanced mechanical control-center boss arena;
- a shortened return route;
- teleportation back to the Ark;
- a compromised Ark final arena;
- visible dormant bodies during the final confrontation;
- opening pods during the ending.

## Scope Boundaries

The World Bible does not need to define:

- the complete planet;
- the origin of the megastructure;
- every former civilization;
- multiple branching routes;
- open-world exploration;
- large outdoor regions;
- settlements unrelated to the playable route;
- extensive environmental simulation;
- alternate campaign paths;
- procedural stage generation.

Additional world detail should be added only when its absence would materially reduce implementation clarity or the coherence of the playable route.
