# Narrative Specification

Status: Approved

## Purpose

This document defines the minimum narrative foundation required to implement a complete, self-contained story for Irys+Ion.

The narrative exists to support the game’s combat, roguelite structure, environments, major reveals, and ending.

It should not require extensive external lore, sequel setup, encyclopedic history, or prolonged exposition.

The game should contain everything necessary to understand its central story.

## Narrative Principles

The story is complete and self-contained.

The game may imply a larger world through environments, brief dialogue, and optional collectibles, but the condition and history of the entire planet do not need to be exhaustively defined.

Narrative delivery should remain sparse.

The game should rely primarily on:

- repeated play;
- environmental evidence;
- the Ark preparation sequence;
- short dialogue exchanges;
- a small number of major recontextualizing reveals;
- the final confrontation and ending.

The narrative should not interrupt the game repeatedly to explain its own lore.

## Ion

Ion is the persistent intelligence embodied as the player’s blade.

Ion requires a physical wielder.

Ion created the Ark so that new wielders could be produced and repeated attempts could continue.

Ion persists across runs.

When a selected wielder is defeated, Ion survives, rises above the fallen body, and performs a brief visual transition implying travel back to the Ark.

Ion then binds to another selected body for the next attempt.

## The Ark

The Ark is the roguelite hub and the source of the created Irys bodies.

It is operated by an intelligence whose name is Irys.

The fact that Irys is the Ark intelligence is not explained at the beginning of the game.

The player may initially assume that Irys is the name of the individual body wielding Ion.

The Ark contains thousands of dormant created bodies held in pods.

The large field of pods should be visible during Ark sequences so its scale is established visually rather than through exposition.

## Created Irys Bodies

Each run uses a newly created physical substrate capable of wielding Ion.

There is no memory, identity, or consciousness continuity between one body and the next.

Ion is the continuing intelligence across attempts.

Created bodies vary substantially in anatomy, proportions, physical capability, and gameplay-relevant statistics.

Possible variations include defects or anomalies such as:

- missing limbs;
- additional limbs;
- unusual proportions;
- asymmetry;
- altered mass;
- altered mobility;
- altered durability;
- altered Technique performance.

The full gameplay system governing this variation belongs to the Progression Specification.

Narratively, these differences are physical consequences of the Ark producing many distinct potential wielders.

Every generated body must remain capable of expressing The Language.

## Ark Preparation Sequence

Before each run, the Ark presents three generated Irys candidates in active pods.

The player may inspect:

- each candidate’s visible body;
- anatomical anomalies;
- gameplay-relevant traits;
- derived statistics;
- meaningful advantages and disadvantages.

The player selects one candidate for the run.

There is no unlimited rerolling.

The two unselected pods close, and those candidates remain dormant within the Ark.

The player’s choice is adaptation among the available bodies rather than repeated generation until an ideal body appears.

## Run Failure

When the active Irys body is defeated:

1. The body falls and remains dead.
2. Ion rises above it.
3. A brief transition implies Ion’s return to the Ark.
4. The Ark presents a new group of candidates.
5. The player selects another body.
6. A new attempt begins.

Nothing from the defeated body is transferred into the next body.

There is no general narrative continuity between created wielders.

The player’s knowledge and Ion’s persistence provide continuity across runs.

## Dialogue Presentation

Dialogue is not conventionally voiced.

Words appear in a text box while a tonal electrical signal modulates as the text is revealed.

This presentation language applies to all speaking intelligences.

Different speakers may use distinct:

- tonal signatures;
- signal rhythms;
- text treatment;
- visual modulation.

Dialogue should remain concise.

The electrical signal communicates voice and presence without conventional spoken performance.

## Dialogue Across Runs

Each run may begin with a brief dialogue exchange or signal.

Run failure may also receive a short exchange before Ion returns to the Ark.

Dialogue should draw from small contextual pools rather than repeating one identical line indefinitely.

The planetary AI recognizes Ion as the same persistent intelligence across every run.

Its dialogue may reflect:

- how many times Ion has returned;
- how far the current attempt progressed;
- repeated tactics;
- growing recognition;
- irritation;
- strategic engagement.

Contact from the planetary AI is sparse during early stages.

Its presence becomes more frequent and direct as the player advances.

## The Planetary AI

The planetary AI is the central antagonist.

Its complete origin, ideology, and planetary history do not need to be exhaustively explained.

The game needs only enough information to establish:

- it controls the territory Ion must cross;
- Ion is attempting to reach and defeat it;
- it recognizes Ion across successive bodies;
- it is attempting to locate and destroy the Ark;
- it cannot initially reach the Ark directly;
- repeated observation of Ion’s returns gradually reveals the Ark’s location or access path.

The planetary AI should not behave as though every run is Ion’s first attempt.

## Campaign Structure

The game is a boss-focused linear gauntlet.

Every run begins fresh at the Ark.

The player selects one of three generated Irys candidates and then proceeds through the complete fixed boss sequence.

Defeating a boss does not permanently unlock a later starting point, checkpoint, shortcut, or boss skip.

The boss roster does not rotate between runs.

The route does not branch.

The complete run structure is:

1. Ark preparation and Irys selection.
2. First outward stage and first guardian boss.
3. Second outward stage and second guardian boss.
4. Third outward stage and third guardian boss.
5. Planetary AI control center and the large technological boss.
6. Return route to the Ark.
7. Final confrontation at the Ark.

The connective stages should be short relative to the boss encounters.

They exist to:

- establish the next environment;
- introduce or rehearse mechanics relevant to the upcoming boss;
- provide sparse environmental storytelling;
- deliver planetary AI dialogue;
- create pacing between major encounters.

They should contain a small number of deliberate enemies rather than repeated waves of disposable opposition.

Boss encounters should account for most of the game’s active combat time.

The primary progression across runs is:

- player mastery of the fixed encounters;
- adaptation to the selected Irys body;
- narrative recognition of Ion’s repeated attempts.

Dialogue at the beginning of a run, at boss arrival, after boss victory, and at run failure may reflect the deepest point reached during the previous attempt.

The next created Irys does not remember that progress.

Ion and the planetary AI do.

## Narrative Recontextualizations

The story should contain a small number of major revelations.

### Repeated Attempts

The player comes to understand that Ion has made many previous attempts using different created bodies.

Environmental remains, prior damage, failed routes, repeated dialogue, and the Ark loop may communicate this without a dedicated exposition scene.

### The Identity of Irys

The player initially has reason to associate the name Irys with the individual wielding body.

The later reveal establishes that Irys is the intelligence operating the Ark.

The created bodies are products of Irys rather than a continuing person repeatedly restored.

### The Ark Has Been Found

Ion believes the Ark remains outside the planetary AI’s direct reach.

Repeated returns allow the planetary AI to observe and eventually trace Ion.

After the apparent defeat of the planetary AI’s control-center guardian, it reveals that it has already reached the Ark.

A key line may be presented substantially as:

> I’ve seen you come and go a thousand times. How long did you think you could stay hidden from me?

The roguelite loop itself therefore creates the vulnerability that enables the final attack.

### The Mirrored Final Form

For the final confrontation, the planetary AI assumes a blade-form analogous to Ion.

It binds to or controls another created Irys body at the Ark.

The player confronts an opposing configuration that mirrors Ion and its wielder.

The opposing body is not the true antagonist.

The blade is the planetary AI itself.

Defeating the opposing wielder defeats the planetary AI because it has committed itself to that embodied blade-form.

## Boss Progression

The fixed boss sequence expresses the planetary AI’s attempt to adapt to Ion’s repeated infiltration success.

The planetary AI observes which defensive forms fail.

Its guardians increasingly converge on the Irys–Ion configuration as it recognizes that a mobile wielder paired with a blade intelligence is the superior infiltration and combat model.

This progression is authored into the fixed boss roster.

It is not a procedural system that redesigns bosses after each failed run.

### First Guardian

The first guardian is crude, mechanical, industrial, or otherwise strongly non-Irys-like.

It should clearly read as an early defensive solution.

Its encounter should test the player’s basic use of The Language.

### Second Guardian

The second guardian is more articulated, mobile, and adaptive.

It may contain early hints of humanoid geometry without closely resembling Irys.

It represents the planetary AI moving away from crude machinery toward a more capable combat form.

### Third Guardian

The third guardian is a large and recognizably Irys-like figure.

It is attached to, restrained against, or integrated into the background environment.

The encounter centers on fighting its arms, legs, or both rather than engaging it as a conventionally mobile opponent.

It is an incomplete or unsuccessful attempt to reproduce the Irys form.

Its resemblance should clearly foreshadow the final mirrored confrontation.

### Control-Center Boss

The control-center boss is a formidable advanced mech or other highly technological combat body.

It represents the planetary AI’s apparent technical apex.

It should feel like a plausible final boss when first encountered.

Its defeat creates the false impression that the planetary AI has been overcome.

### Final Ark Boss

The final boss is a highly Irys-like opposing wielder carrying the planetary AI in blade-form.

This is the culmination of the planetary AI’s observations of Ion.

It has adopted the same fundamental structure:

- a created wielder;
- a persistent blade intelligence;
- a compact directional combat relationship.

The opposing Irys is not the true antagonist.

The blade is the planetary AI itself.

## Final Act

### Penultimate Stage: Control Center

After defeating the three outward guardians, Ion and the current Irys penetrate the planetary AI’s control center.

The stage culminates in the advanced technological control-center boss.

This boss should appear to be the planetary AI’s ultimate defensive form.

Defeating that boss appears to complete the mission.

### Reversal

The planetary AI reveals that the control-center boss was not its final embodiment.

Repeated observation of Ion’s returns allowed it to trace the route back to the Ark.

It has reached the Ark by exploiting the connection exposed through Ion’s repeated travel.

It has activated, seized, or bound itself to one of the Ark’s created Irys bodies.

The planetary AI has assumed a blade-form analogous to Ion.

A key line may be presented substantially as:

> I’ve seen you come and go a thousand times. How long did you think you could stay hidden from me?

### Return Stage

The current Irys and Ion must travel back to the Ark.

The return route should be shorter and more urgent than the outward journey.

Previously familiar spaces may be altered, destabilized, bypassed, or recontextualized.

The return should not require replaying the complete outward boss sequence in reverse.

### Final Stage: The Ark

The Ark changes from a place of safety, selection, and renewal into the final battlefield.

The final confrontation is:

- the player’s current Irys wielding Ion;
- another created Irys wielding the planetary AI in blade-form.

The opposing configuration is the planetary AI’s final conclusion after observing Ion’s repeated success.

Defeating the opposing Irys defeats the planetary AI because it has committed itself to that embodied blade-form.

## Ending

The final conflict critically compromises the Ark.

The dormant created bodies cannot be preserved through the Ark’s existing system.

Irys, the Ark intelligence, and Ion merge into one consciousness.

That merged consciousness fractures and distributes itself across the Ark’s created bodies.

The resulting Irys are not one consciousness controlling many bodies.

They awaken as independent individuals.

The bodies that had previously existed as replaceable substrates, candidates, defects, and failed possibilities become people.

The final sequence should include one of the earliest unselected Irys candidates opening her pod.

Recognizing that body provides a direct visual payoff to the Ark selection sequence and confirms that the dormant candidates now possess independent lives.

The Ark’s cycle of producing disposable wielders ends.

The planetary AI is defeated.

The central story is resolved without requiring a sequel hook.

## Scope Boundaries

The Narrative Specification does not need to define:

- a complete planetary history;
- every former civilization;
- extensive faction genealogies;
- a mythology designed for external lore analysis;
- detailed biographies for every created body;
- the planetary AI’s full philosophical doctrine;
- sequel setup.

Additional lore should be added only when its absence would materially reduce implementation clarity, environmental coherence, or the impact of the central reveals.

## Implementation Requirements

The implemented game must communicate:

- Ion persists across runs.
- Created Irys bodies do not share memory or identity.
- The Ark produces varied potential wielders.
- Three candidates can be inspected before each run.
- Unselected candidates remain dormant.
- Ion visibly returns to the Ark after failure.
- Every run begins fresh at the Ark.
- Every run uses the same fixed linear boss sequence.
- Bosses cannot be permanently skipped.
- The route does not branch.
- The boss roster does not rotate.
- Boss encounters account for most active combat time.
- Connective stages remain short and use low enemy density.
- Dialogue may reference the deepest progress of the previous run.
- Dialogue uses text with modulated electrical tones.
- The planetary AI recognizes Ion across runs.
- Dialogue frequency increases as stages progress.
- The planetary AI’s guardians increasingly resemble Irys.
- The progression from crude machinery to the anti-Irys reflects its observation of Ion.
- The boss progression is fixed and authored rather than procedurally adaptive.
- The third outward boss is a large restrained or environmentally integrated proto-Irys.
- The control-center boss is an advanced technological mech and false final boss.
- The planetary AI traces Ion’s repeated returns to the Ark.
- The true final confrontation occurs at the Ark.
- The planetary AI appears in blade-form wielded by another Irys.
- Irys is revealed as the Ark intelligence.
- Irys and Ion merge after victory.
- The merged consciousness divides across the dormant bodies.
- The awakened Irys become independent individuals.
- The story ends completely.
